ICT in Practice 2020

Researchers Olaf Janssen, Bernd-Jan Witkamp and Tom Langhorst presented the outcomes of the Nano4Sports research initiative at the SECSI 2019 Science & Engineering Conference at Eindhoven University of Technology. Their findings are documented in a concise research paper that is part of the published conference proceedings. On February 7th 2020 at Strijp-TQ in Eindhoven de Nano4Sports research project was officially completed with an all-day demonstration and a word of appreciation by professor Mark de Graaf, head of the research chair Interaction Design. The Nano4Sports demonstration was part of the programme of the ICT In Practice 2020 conference at Strijp TQ.

Project transfer

Overall Mark de Graaf concluded that the results of the research project were fully satisfactory and that Nao4Sports lived up to its expectations. The findings and methods of Nano4Sports will be transfered to the continuing The Vitality living lab project of the research chair Interaction Design. That project has a projected end date in Spring 2021.

The Nano4Sports paper can be found in the Proceedings of the 4th Science & Engineering Conference on Sports Innovation.

ICT in Practice 2019

Upload Date

Mar 27, 2019

3 Collaborators

Tom Langhorst

Bernd-Jan Witkamp

Olaf Janssen

External Resources

The team of researchers of the Nano4Sports project presented three demos at the ICT in Practice symposium at Natlab, Eindhoven, on Thursday the 21st of March. The demos showed that surprising progress has been made in the previous few months. The Nano4Sports laboratory is in use since the beginning of 2019.

Sensor System

The first demo showed a system to accurately identify steps by using custom sensors. This information is the essential basis to analyse if a runner or walker is moving in an ergonomical way. The next demo showed the use of smartphones and AI algorithms to classify a moving pattern as walking or running.

Step Sonification

In the last demo directional audio was used to correct the posture of runners. If the body of a runner is off balance, audio signals motivate the runner to correct his posture. This is a new technique that has not been used before.

Test Lab Fully Operational

Upload Date

Feb 19, 2019

3 Collaborators

Bernd-Jan Witkamp

Olaf Janssen

Tom Langhorst

External Resources

The centre of expertise Interaction Design IXD took one step forward as the new testing environment at Fontys School of ICT at Strijp-TQ, Eindhoven, is now fully operational. Runners can use the treadmill. Speakers are reflecting on their movements with sounds and body sensors measure the effect of that feedback.

The new sonification laboratory was created with the help of research partner InnoSportLab Sport & Beweeg! The equipment seems rather squeezed in into a room at Strijp-TQ, but the new lab has exactly all that is needed.

Towards translating the effect of music into motor using deep learning

Upload Date

Sep 25, 2019

3 Collaborators

Olaf Janssen

Tom Langhorst

Bernd-Jan Witkamp

External Resources

ResearchGate

Abstract

Artificial neural networks can be used to detect emotion from gait and music can act as an agent to influence gait. While the impact of specific musical composition elements may be highly personal (every person has their own preferred running playlist), we hypothesize that using deep learning, it becomes possible to steer individual running technique into a controlled direction by exposing the runner to music with the appropriate musical parameters. To test our hypothesis, we designed a versatile sensing and sonification platform and describe our experimental approach.
